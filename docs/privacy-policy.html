Privacy Policy — GPX Editor

Last updated: 20 June 2026

GPX Editor (the “App”) is an offline-first tool for editing GPS track files (GPX, FIT, TCX, KML). This policy explains what data the App handles and how. By using the App you agree to this policy.

The short version: Your track files are processed entirely on your device and are never uploaded. We don’t ask for your name, email, or any account. To keep the App free and to improve it, Google’s advertising (AdMob) and analytics (Firebase) services collect some device and usage data, described below. Network use is limited to map tiles, ads, and anonymous usage analytics.

1. Your GPS files stay on your device

All importing, merging, trimming, repairing, converting, and analysis happens locally on your device. The App does not transmit your track files, location history, or any file contents to our servers — we operate no servers that receive your data. Files you save or share go only where you choose (a folder you pick, or an app you select in the system share sheet).

2. Data we do not collect

  • We require no account and no sign-in.
  • We do not collect your name, email, or contacts.
  • We do not build a profile of your rides, and your track-file contents are never sent off your device.

The advertising and analytics services described below do process some device and usage data — see sections 4 and 5.

3. Maps (OpenStreetMap)

When a map is shown, the App downloads map tiles from the OpenStreetMap tile servers. This necessarily sends your IP address and the map area being viewed to those servers. See the OpenStreetMap Foundation Privacy Policy.

4. Advertising (Google AdMob)

The App is free and supported by ads served through Google AdMob. To show ads, the Google Mobile Ads SDK may collect and process device and usage information — including an advertising identifier, IP address, and ad-interaction data — in accordance with Google’s policies. This data is handled by Google, not by us.

  • How Google uses data: policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites.
  • In the EEA, UK, and Switzerland, the App shows a Google-certified consent message (UMP) before serving personalised ads, and respects your choice.
  • You can reset or limit ad tracking via your device settings (Android: Settings → Google → Ads).

5. Usage analytics (Google Firebase)

To understand which features are used and to improve the App, we use Google Analytics for Firebase. It records anonymous, aggregated usage events — for example, which tool you open and whether you share or save a file — together with basic device information and an app-instance / advertising identifier assigned by Google. We use this only in aggregate to prioritise improvements; it is not linked to your name or used to identify you, and your track-file contents are never included in these events.

  • How Google handles Firebase data: firebase.google.com/support/privacy.
  • You can limit analytics tied to the advertising ID via Android Settings → Google → Ads.

6. Permissions

The App requests only the INTERNET permission, used solely for the map tiles, ads, and usage analytics described above. It does not request access to your device location, contacts, camera, or microphone. Files are opened and saved through the system file picker, which grants access only to the specific files you choose.

7. Children

The App is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the App evolves. Material changes will be reflected by the “Last updated” date above.

9. Contact

Questions about this policy? Email contact@velologic-labs.eu.
